About Our Initiative

The Mobile Tech Hub Initiative is a student-led project aimed at bridging the digital divide between rural and urban communities in Minnesota.

Our initiative comes from three undergraduate students at the University of North Dakota who understand the challenges faced by rural communities. We noticed a significant gap in technological opportunities between rural and urban children, and we believe we can make a difference.

By implementing a four-day school week and deploying mobile tech vans equipped with high-tech devices, we aim to provide rural students with access to technology they might not otherwise experience. Our goal is to inspire a new generation of tech-savvy youth who can thrive in their own communities.

The Digital Divide

Rural students face unique challenges in accessing modern technology and developing digital literacy skills.

Students in the Win-E-Mac School system and surrounding rural areas face significant challenges in accessing modern technology due to:

  • Limited school budgets due to small student populations
  • Inadequate equipment and technological infrastructure
  • Slow, outdated, or nonexistent internet connections
  • Lack of exposure to advanced technology tools and STEM resources
  • Geographical isolation that limits access to tech-focused educational opportunities

This digital divide restricts rural students' ability to develop critical skills and interest in technology, potentially hindering their future career prospects and widening the gap with their urban peers.

Our Solution

We're bringing technology directly to rural students through mobile tech hubs and innovative scheduling.

Mobile Tech Hubs

Vans equipped with laptops, Wi-Fi hotspots, robotics kits, and cybersecurity research tools that travel to schools on a regular schedule.

Four-Day School Week

A streamlined school schedule with the fifth day dedicated to distance learning, allowing students more one-on-one time with their devices.

Tech Education

Staffed by knowledgeable tech educators who help students overcome challenges and develop their own technology projects.

Benefits for Students and Communities

Our initiative offers numerous advantages for rural students, schools, and communities.

Improved Tech Skills

Students gain hands-on experience with modern technology, developing digital literacy and STEM skills.

Cost-Effective Sharing

Resources are shared across multiple schools, making advanced technology more affordable for rural districts.

Reduced School Costs

Four-day school weeks lead to approximately 2% savings on transportation, energy, and operational expenses.

Increased Engagement

Students develop positive attitudes toward technology and education, with something exciting to look forward to each week.

Community Retention

By empowering rural youth with tech skills, we help them innovate locally without leaving for urban areas.

Growth Mindset

Exposure to new technology promotes intellectual curiosity and a positive approach to learning challenges.
